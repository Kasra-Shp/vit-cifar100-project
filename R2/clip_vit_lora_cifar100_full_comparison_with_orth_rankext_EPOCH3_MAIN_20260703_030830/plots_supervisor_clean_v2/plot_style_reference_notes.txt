Copied/adapted visual style:
- Large white figures, bold titles, simple Matplotlib colors, thicker lines, and no point markers.
- Family separation: Simple-Average Family and Rank-Extension Family are plotted separately, matching the good paper-style CE plots.
- CL-step separation is explicit. Because the latest run has only epoch-level logs, loss plots use local epoch 1/2/3 inside each CL step and do not connect across task boundaries.
- Legends are outside the axes to avoid covering curves.
- Light horizontal grid lines are used; x-axis labels are local epochs or CL-step facets.
- Factor-Orth and total-loss plots use log scale where needed to handle large regularizer/total-loss ranges without flattening CE-scale methods.

Why these plots are easier to interpret than the previous jagged version:
- The previous clean plots connected sparse global epoch labels across the full 15-epoch sequence and used markers, making each method look like a sharp sawtooth.
- The v2 plots do not draw lines across CL task boundaries. Each step is shown as its own local 3-epoch mini-curve, so task resets are visually separated instead of implied as continuous dynamics.
- Methods are grouped into Simple-Average vs Rank-Extension families, reducing legend/curve overload.
- The combined decomposition separates CE, KD, Factor-Orth, and Total components into component facets instead of forcing incompatible scales into one axis.
- All plotted values are directly read from the latest R2 CSV files; final accuracy metrics and logged losses were not changed.
