================================================================================
RANK_EXTENSION FAMILY: FIRST_STEP ACCURACY AND FACTOR-ORTH ANALYSIS
================================================================================
Runs compared:
  BASELINE = R3/results_EPOCH6_20260711_light/.../MAIN_20260711_214100
             EPOCHS=6, target_modules=[q,v]_proj, no classifier calibration,
             no head-LR multiplier (=1.0 implicit), lora_dropout=0.10,
             pre-factor-orth-fix, no best-epoch selection (final epoch used).
  NEW      = R3/results_calibfix_20260716_light/.../MAIN_20260716_133338
             EPOCHS=9, target_modules=[q,k,v,out]_proj, classifier calibration
             ON for simple_avg family / OFF for rank_extension family
             (family-conditional, confirmed in configs/hyperparameters_by_method.json),
             head_lr_multiplier=10.0 (uniform, all 8 methods),
             lora_dropout=0.05, corrected factor-orth formula on the simple_avg
             path only (rank_extension's formula was already correct and is
             confirmed unchanged: lambda_factor_orth=50.0 in both runs, same as
             simple_avg's), best-epoch selection ON (selected_epoch used for
             reporting per method/step; see best_epoch_selected_by_method_step.csv).

Many settings changed simultaneously between these runs (epochs, modules,
dropout, head-LR, calibration policy). Where a specific cause cannot be
isolated from a single changed variable, this report says so explicitly
rather than assigning single-cause blame.

PRIOR CONTEXT (analysis_rankext_drop/, 2026-07-15): an earlier run
(results_4mod_20260715_light) had calibration ON for ALL methods including
rank_extension, and that was diagnosed as the primary cause of a KD-variant
collapse in that run. The NEW run analyzed here (results_calibfix_20260716)
is the confirmation run after disabling calibration for rank_extension
specifically. That fix is CONFIRMED WORKING: rank_extension_kd_only_T2 and
rank_extension_orth_factor_lam_50_kd_T2 both recovered to within a few points
of BASELINE's all_seen accuracy (63.51 vs 59.29, and 62.89 vs 67.98) instead
of the catastrophic 50.12 / 26.24 seen with calibration mistakenly applied to
rank_extension. The finding in THIS report — a new, near-total first_step
collapse specific to rank_extension_orth_factor_lam_50 (non-KD) — is a
DIFFERENT, previously undocumented phenomenon that appears only once
calibration is correctly turned off, and is not explained by H2 from that
earlier analysis.

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
================================================================================
TARGET 1 — first_step accuracy does NOT drop uniformly across the
rank_extension family. Two of four variants IMPROVED (plain RankExt +8.2pp,
RankExt+KD +6.8pp); only the two factor-orth variants declined, and only the
non-KD one collapsed catastrophically (RankExt+FactorOrth: 8.35% -> 0.10%,
loss 5.75 -> 11.39; RankExt+FactorOrth+KD: a mild -2.15pp). The premise "the
family drops" should be narrowed to "the factor-orth variants drop, severely
for non-KD."

The strongest, cleanest finding in this whole analysis: for ALL FOUR variants
in BOTH runs, step-1's own training converges to a low, healthy validation CE
(~0.14-0.21) and this value is IDENTICAL across all 4 method variants within
a run, because orth/KD mechanisms are structurally inactive during step 1
(no prior blocks to be orthogonal to, no teacher to distill from yet). Yet the
FINAL first_step evaluation loss (measured after all 5 CL steps finish)
ranges 0.70-11.39 -- a 4.8x to 68.7x inflation over the training-time value.
This proves the degradation is a FORGETTING-TIME phenomenon (something that
happens to step-1's knowledge during steps 2-5), not a training-time
phenomenon. This holds for every method in every run, including the ones that
improved -- rank_extension's first-step forgetting is a pre-existing property
of the method, not something newly introduced.

TARGET 2 — the thesis "factor-orth helps simple_avg but is redundant-to-
harmful for rank_extension" is PARTIALLY supported, but the picture is more
specific and time-dependent than a flat statement: in BASELINE, factor-orth
was a net POSITIVE for rank_extension too (all_seen +11.5pp non-KD, +8.7pp
KD) -- just a smaller positive than for simple_avg. It only flips to net
NEGATIVE in NEW (all_seen -5.7pp non-KD, -0.6pp KD), and the flip is almost
entirely attributable to the catastrophic first_step collapse of the non-KD
variant (-71pp vs its simple_avg counterpart in NEW, vs only -7.6pp behind in
BASELINE). Structurally, the orth penalty IS close to redundant for
rank_extension in both runs -- its magnitude relative to CE at the moment it
could matter is 20-300x smaller than for simple_avg, because a freshly
initialized new-rank block starts close to orthogonal to the frozen old
blocks by construction, so there is little violation to correct. But
"redundant" is not the same as "harmless": in both runs, adding factor-orth
measurably enlarges the transient training-CE spike at the start of every new
CL step relative to plain rank_extension, and this relative penalty GROWS
with CL step index (more frozen blocks to respect) and is *more pronounced in
NEW than in BASELINE* (step-5 CE ratio orth/plain: 2.48x in BASELINE vs 3.37x
in NEW). NEW differs from BASELINE in exactly two ways that plausibly explain
why the same underlying tension tips over into catastrophic failure only in
NEW: 4 target modules (roughly doubling the number of simultaneous
orthogonality constraints per step) and head_lr_multiplier=10 (which was
absent in BASELINE and would amplify whatever noisy classifier-head gradient
this transient spike produces). This causal chain is plausible and consistent
with all available evidence, but cannot be fully proven from these saved CSVs
alone (no per-row classifier weight norms are logged) -- it is reported as
the best-supported hypothesis, not a certainty.

================================================================================
TARGET 1 -- FIRST_STEP ACCURACY IN THE RANK_EXTENSION FAMILY
================================================================================

1.0  QUANTIFYING THE DROP (table2_rankext_perstep_and_forgetting.csv,
     fig1_first_step_accuracy_deltas.png)

  method                      BASELINE    NEW      delta      later_steps delta
  --------------------------------------------------------------------------
  rank_extension                 3.75%   11.95%   +8.20 pp         +19.25 pp
  rank_extension_kd_only_T2     69.40%   76.20%   +6.80 pp          +3.58 pp
  rank_extension_orth_factor_50  8.35%    0.10%   -8.25 pp          +1.83 pp
  rank_extension_orth_50_kd_T2  80.30%   78.15%   -2.15 pp          -5.83 pp

  Only the two factor-orth variants declined; the non-KD one is a
  near-total collapse (loss 5.75 -> 11.39, i.e. the model is effectively
  outputting near-zero probability mass on the correct class for step-1's
  test images once all 5 steps have finished).

1.a  DOES FIRST-STEP DEGRADATION CORRELATE WITH MORE LATER-STEP LEARNING?
     (classic forgetting: more adaptation capacity in NEW interferes more
     with frozen step-1 representations)

  NOT SUPPORTED as a family-wide explanation. If more later-step learning
  drove more step-1 interference, the method with the LARGEST later_steps
  gain should show the worst first_step outcome. Instead:
    - plain rank_extension has the single largest later_steps gain of the
      four variants (+19.25pp) and its first_step IMPROVED (+8.2pp).
    - rank_extension_orth_factor_lam_50 -- the one with the catastrophic
      first_step collapse -- has almost no later_steps change (+1.83pp),
      the smallest movement of any of the four variants.
  The data show no positive correlation between later-step learning gains
  and first-step degradation across this family; if anything the direction
  is opposite for the two extremes. Hypothesis (a) is REJECTED as the
  primary driver of the observed collapse.

1.b  CLASSIFIER / HEAD-LR x10 EFFECT: DO STEP-1 CLASSIFIER ROWS GET
     OVERWRITTEN AS LATER STEPS TRAIN?

  Two config facts, confirmed directly from configs/hyperparameters_by_method.json
  (table7_hyperparameter_confirmation.csv):
    - apply_calibration is FALSE for all four rank_extension variants in NEW
      (and calibration did not exist at all in BASELINE) -- so the post-hoc
      classifier row-norm calibration mechanism that was the confirmed
      culprit in the PRIOR run (results_4mod_20260715_light) is NOT active
      here and cannot explain this collapse.
    - head_lr_multiplier=10.0 is applied UNIFORMLY to all 8 methods in NEW,
      including the two rank_extension variants that IMPROVED. If a 10x
      head LR alone were sufficient to corrupt step-1's classifier rows via
      overwriting, all four rank_extension variants should show degradation.
      They don't -- half improved substantially.
  So head-LR x10 alone is REJECTED as a sufficient cause. It remains a
  plausible AMPLIFIER of whatever destabilizing signal is specific to the
  orth variant (see Target 2 below) -- a 10x-larger classifier learning rate
  would turn a modest transient loss spike into a much larger one-shot
  weight change -- but it cannot be the trigger by itself since it is
  identical across all four variants and only two of them fail.
  Forgetting/backward-transfer columns (final_metrics_all_methods.csv, NEW
  run) are populated for rank_extension: forgetting_metric is 0.679 (plain)
  vs 0.752 (orth, both runs -- BASELINE 0.7516 and NEW 0.7524, essentially
  unchanged) -- interestingly this run-level metric does NOT show the same
  dramatic jump that first_step accuracy shows for the orth variant. This
  suggests the metric (likely averaged over multiple steps/tasks) is not
  sensitive enough to flag the specific first_step catastrophe in isolation,
  and should not be read as contradicting the accuracy numbers.

1.c  TRAINING-TIME VS FORGETTING-TIME CAUSE (table3_step1_trainingtime_
     vs_evaltime.csv, fig2_step1_trainingtime_vs_forgetting.png)

  THIS IS THE CLEANEST RESULT IN THE ANALYSIS. Step 1 is the same
  optimization problem for all 4 rank_extension variants (no KD teacher, no
  prior block to be orthogonal to), and the per-epoch training history
  confirms this exactly: BASELINE step-1 training ends at val_ce=0.1457
  (best across step-1 epochs: 0.1380) for all four variants; NEW step-1
  training ends at val_ce=0.2073 (best-epoch-selected: 0.1657) for all four
  variants. These are healthy, convergent, unremarkable numbers.

  Compare to the FINAL first_step evaluation loss (measured after all 5 CL
  steps finish training):
    method                          BASELINE final   NEW final   NEW inflation*
    rank_extension                       6.98            5.88         35.5x
    rank_extension_kd_only_T2            0.95            0.80          4.8x
    rank_extension_orth_factor_lam_50    5.75           11.39         68.7x
    rank_extension_orth_factor_lam_50_kd 0.70            0.82          4.9x
    (*NEW final loss / NEW step-1 training-time best val_ce of 0.1657)

  Every method-run combination shows a large loss inflation (4.8x-68.7x)
  between "how well the step-1 model scored right after training on step 1"
  and "how well that knowledge survives to the end of the full 5-step run."
  This is true even for the methods that IMPROVED. Conclusion: rank_extension
  first-step forgetting is a FORGETTING-TIME phenomenon happening during
  steps 2-5, not a training-time convergence problem at step 1 -- in every
  run, every variant. What changed between BASELINE and NEW is not whether
  this forgetting happens (it always does), but how SEVERE it is for one
  specific variant (rank_extension_orth_factor_lam_50: 39.5x in BASELINE ->
  68.7x in NEW, the worst inflation of all 8 method-run combinations).

1.d  RANKED HYPOTHESES (most to least supported by evidence)

  1. [CONFIRMED, applies to the whole family] First-step degradation is a
     forgetting-time effect (steps 2-5 corrupting step-1's already-converged
     state), not a training-time effect. Directly evidenced by table3: step-1
     training is uniformly healthy and near-identical across variants and
     runs; only the post-hoc, cross-step evaluation loss diverges.
  2. [WELL-SUPPORTED, specific to the orth variant] Factor-orth introduces an
     extra, growing transient CE spike at every step boundary for
     rank_extension (see Target 2 below in full), and this spike is larger in
     NEW than BASELINE at the later steps. Combined with head_lr x10 (new to
     this run, applied uniformly) and 4 target modules (also new, doubling
     the number of simultaneous orthogonality constraints), this plausibly
     explains why the SAME structural forgetting-time weakness of
     rank_extension became catastrophic specifically for the orth+non-KD
     variant in NEW while it was mildly beneficial in BASELINE. Plausible and
     consistent with all evidence gathered, but not provable from these CSVs
     alone (no per-row classifier weight logs available).
  3. [REJECTED as primary cause] More later-step learning capacity causing
     more interference (hypothesis a) -- contradicted by the method with the
     largest later-steps gain (plain rank_extension) also having the largest
     first-step improvement, not degradation.
  4. [REJECTED as sufficient cause] head_lr x10 alone (hypothesis b) --
     applied uniformly to all 4 variants, but only 2 of 4 collapsed. Cannot
     be the sole trigger, though it remains a plausible amplifier (see #2).
     Classifier row-norm calibration (H2 from the prior 2026-07-15 analysis)
     is confirmed OFF for rank_extension in this run and is therefore ruled
     out entirely as an explanation here.

TARGET 2 -- WHY RANK_EXTENSION + FACTOR_ORTH REMAINS WEAK
================================================================================

2.a  FACTOR-ORTH LOSS TRAJECTORIES: RANK_EXT VS SIMPLE_AVG
     (table4_factororth_trajectory_stats.csv, fig3_factororth_trajectories_
     by_family.png)

  lambda_factor_orth=50.0 is confirmed IDENTICAL for both families (not a
  lambda-mismatch artifact; table7). At the start of each new CL step
  (local_epoch=1, step>1), factor_orth_loss_weighted relative to train_ce_loss
  (factor_orth_weighted_over_ce):

    rank_extension_orth_factor_lam_50:  BASELINE 1.8x, 3.2x, 60.9x*, 4.6x
                                         NEW      2.8x, 3.0x, 3.1x, 4.1x
    simple_avg_factor_orth:             BASELINE 103x, 401x, 768x, 858x
                                         NEW      149x, 672x, 1226x, 1412x
    (*BASELINE step-4 rank_ext value is a single-point outlier driven by an
     unusually large raw orth violation that step, 7.23 vs ~0.2-0.7 at the
     other three step transitions -- plausibly random-seed variance in that
     step's new-block initialization, not a systematic pattern.)

  Excluding that one outlier, rank_extension's orth penalty is consistently
  ~20-300x SMALLER relative to CE than simple_avg's, in both runs. The decay
  shape is similar (both collapse ~2-4 orders of magnitude within 2 epochs
  of a step starting -- fig3 shows near-identical shapes on a log scale) but
  simple_avg's peaks are always three orders of magnitude higher in absolute
  terms. Read plainly: the orth penalty barely engages for rank_extension
  in either run.

2.b  DOES FACTOR-ORTH MAKE CONCEPTUAL SENSE FOR RANK_EXT? REDUNDANT OR
     ACTIVELY HARMFUL?

  rank_extension already enforces block separation structurally: each CL
  step adds a fresh, randomly-initialized 16-dim rank block
  (rankext_new_rank_per_step=16, schedule 16->32->48->64->80) while old
  blocks remain frozen but active in the forward pass
  (old_active_in_forward=True). A random fresh subspace is, on average,
  close to orthogonal to a fixed prior subspace in a moderately
  high-dimensional weight space -- exactly what 2.a's tiny raw factor_orth
  values at epoch 2+ of every step confirm (down to 1e-4 to 1e-6 by the
  penultimate epoch). So yes: the orthogonality constraint is close to
  REDUNDANT for rank_extension by construction, in the sense that there is
  little violation left for it to correct after the first epoch of each step.

  But redundant is not the same as harmless, and there IS evidence of active
  (if modest) harm at the moment each new step begins, before the redundancy
  kicks in:
    - train_ce_loss at the first epoch of each new step is measurably higher
      for the orth variant than for plain rank_extension, in both runs, at
      every step transition (table4). The ratio grows with step index:
        BASELINE: 1.74x -> 1.96x -> 1.95x -> 2.48x  (steps 2->3->4->5)
        NEW:      1.47x -> 2.02x -> 2.62x -> 3.37x
      NEW's ratio is higher than BASELINE's at every step from step 3 onward,
      and grows faster. This is present in the KD variants too but damped:
        BASELINE KD: 1.52x -> 1.65x -> 1.69x -> 1.74x
        NEW KD:      1.33x -> 1.42x -> 1.41x -> 1.44x
      (KD's distillation loss anchors outputs toward a stable teacher signal,
      which plausibly damps the destabilizing swing that pure factor-orth
      introduces -- consistent with the KD variant suffering "to a lesser
      degree," per Target 2's framing.)
    - Despite this larger transient at step-start, END-of-step convergence
      quality is NOT worse for the orth variant -- table4b shows the orth
      variant's final-epoch val_ce is equal to or BETTER than plain
      rank_extension's at every one of the 5 steps, in both runs (e.g. NEW
      step 5: orth 0.110 vs plain 0.127). So factor-orth does not damage
      each step's own local fit; the harm, if any, is transmitted forward
      into how well that step's training preserves EARLIER steps' knowledge,
      not into the current step's own accuracy.
  Verdict: the data support "redundant AND mildly, transiently harmful at
  step boundaries, growing worse with more CL steps and worse in NEW than
  BASELINE" -- not "purely redundant with zero cost," and not "actively and
  uniformly harmful throughout training" either. It is a narrow, specific
  cost concentrated in the first epoch of each new step.

2.c  QUANTIFIED COST/GAIN OF FACTOR-ORTH VS PLAIN RANK_EXTENSION
     (table5_rankext_vs_rankext_orth_headtohead.csv,
      fig4_rankext_vs_rankext_orth.png)

  non-KD pair (rank_extension vs rank_extension_orth_factor_lam_50):
    BASELINE:  first_step +4.60pp, later_steps +13.28pp, all_seen +11.54pp
               (orth HELPS across the board)
    NEW:       first_step -11.85pp, later_steps -4.15pp, all_seen -5.69pp
               (orth HURTS across the board; first_step is catastrophic)

  KD pair (rank_extension_kd_only_T2 vs rank_extension_orth_factor_lam_50_kd_T2):
    BASELINE:  first_step +10.90pp, later_steps +8.14pp, all_seen +8.69pp
               (orth clearly helps)
    NEW:       first_step +1.95pp, later_steps -1.26pp, all_seen -0.62pp
               (roughly a wash, small net negative)

  Factor-orth's effect on rank_extension REVERSED SIGN between the two runs
  for the non-KD pair, and weakened from clearly-positive to roughly-neutral
  for the KD pair. The magnitude of the reversal (non-KD, all_seen: +11.5pp
  -> -5.7pp, a 17.2pp swing) is far larger than what a "mostly redundant,
  slightly harmful at step boundaries" story alone would predict from 2.a/2.b
  -- the step-boundary CE ratio only grew moderately (2.48x -> 3.37x at step
  5). This gap between the modest training-time signal and the large final
  outcome swing is exactly the same "forgetting-time amplification" pattern
  documented in Target 1.c: a training-time nudge gets compounded/amplified
  somewhere in the cross-step merge and final-evaluation path in ways this
  data can characterize (direction, and rough magnitude of the amplifiers)
  but not fully decompose.

2.d  IS "FACTOR-ORTH HELPS SIMPLE_AVG BUT IS REDUNDANT-TO-HARMFUL FOR
     RANK_EXTENSION" SUPPORTED?

  PARTIALLY, and only for the NEW run. The comparison against simple_avg's
  own factor-orth counterpart (table6_rankext_orth_vs_simpleavg_orth_
  counterpart_gap.csv) shows:

    non-KD, all_seen:  BASELINE rank_ext+orth trails simple_avg+orth by
                        -26.53pp; NEW trails by -40.63pp (gap widens)
    non-KD, first_step: BASELINE rank_ext+orth is AHEAD by +7.55pp (both
                        low: 8.35% vs 0.8%); NEW trails by -71.10pp
                        (catastrophic reversal, driven entirely by the
                        near-zero NEW first_step collapse)
    KD, all_seen:       BASELINE rank_ext+orth AHEAD by +4.18pp; NEW trails
                        by only -0.82pp (small gap either direction --
                        "to a lesser degree," exactly as framed in the task)

  So: in BASELINE, rank_extension's factor-orth variant was NOT clearly
  weaker than simple_avg's -- on all_seen it under-performed on later_steps
  specifically (-35.05pp) but this was offset by strength elsewhere, and the
  KD pair was ahead of its simple_avg counterpart. The "redundant-to-harmful"
  framing only becomes strongly true in NEW, and almost entirely because of
  the single catastrophic first_step collapse in the non-KD variant -- remove
  that one number and the family-level gap (later_steps, all_seen) is
  present but far more modest (-33 / -40.6pp on later/all_seen, similar
  order to BASELINE's -35 / -26.5pp).

  Bottom line: the STRUCTURAL argument for redundancy (2.a/2.b: the orth
  penalty is 20-300x weaker in relative terms for rank_extension, and offers
  little to correct after step 1 by construction) is well-supported and
  consistent across both runs. The "harmful" half of the thesis is
  SUPPORTED ONLY FOR THE NEW RUN, and concentrated almost entirely in a
  single failure mode (non-KD, first_step, NEW) whose exact trigger this
  analysis narrows down to "a growing per-step-transition CE spike, likely
  amplified by the newly-added head_lr x10 and/or the doubled module count,
  compounding across 5 CL steps into eventual near-total classifier
  corruption for step 1" -- but cannot fully prove down to the level of
  individual classifier weight updates from the saved CSVs alone. This
  should be treated as the leading hypothesis for a follow-up run (e.g. one
  with head_lr_multiplier=1.0 restored for rank_extension only, holding
  everything else in NEW fixed, to test whether the collapse disappears).
