================================================================================
analysis_rankext_plain -- candidate_evaluation.txt: five candidate fixes for
plain RankExt's interference, ranked, with one chosen (2026-07-23)
================================================================================
Grounding: diagnosis.txt found (a) frozen LoRA blocks bit-exact, (b) frozen
classifier rows protected by a doubly-redundant, unbroken mechanism, (c) no
weight drift anywhere -- so the ~35+pp restricted-accuracy collapse specific
to plain training must come from how the still-training new block's
perturbation of the SHARED q/v_proj computation interacts with the frozen old
block + frozen old classifier rows, and (d) KD's step-1 rescue is explained
by a concrete anchoring mechanism plain training lacks entirely. Every
candidate is scored against this diagnosis, not in the abstract.

================================================================================
CANDIDATE 1 -- separate (lower) LR for the new rank block
================================================================================
Mechanically: give A_new/B_new their own optimizer parameter group at, e.g.,
0.1x the base LR (classifier head and any other trainable params keep their
existing multiplier -- rank_extension's own head_lr_multiplier is already 1.0,
i.e. no separate group exists for it today). Uniformly slows the new block's
convergence rate across the ENTIRE 9-epoch step, not just the start.

Addresses: partially. A slower-moving new block changes less per step, which
plausibly produces smaller, less disruptive perturbations to the shared
forward computation -- but it does this by slowing convergence everywhere,
not by targeting the specific "early, uninformed, B_new==0-at-start" moment
diagnosis.txt section 4.3 identifies as the likely trigger. It is a blunter
instrument than the actual mechanism calls for.

Expected magnitude: modest and two-sided. Given the existing cosine LR
schedule already anneals LR down over the step, a further 0.1x cut risks
leaving the new block meaningfully UNDER-fit within the fixed 9-epoch/EPOCHS
budget -- i.e. it could trade some of plain RankExt's OWN new-step
(later_steps) accuracy for a partial recovery of old-step retention, not a
clean win on both. No existing data in this codebase directly measures this
tradeoff for rank_extension's new-block LR specifically, so the magnitude is
a plausible-but-unverified guess in either direction.

Risk to other 3 variants: LOW-to-moderate. FactorOrth/KD already have their
own anchor; a slower new block on top would not conflict with those
mechanisms, but by the same "uniformly slower everywhere" logic could shave
some of FactorOrth/KD's currently-fine new-step convergence too, for a
mitigation whose OWN benefit is unproven.

Complexity: LOW -- HeadLRTrainerMixin/build_head_lr_param_groups() already
implements exactly this per-group-multiplier pattern for the classifier head;
extending it to a second group for A_new/B_new is a straightforward reuse.

================================================================================
CANDIDATE 2 -- warmup on the new rank block's LR/output within each step
================================================================================
Mechanically: ramp the new block's effective contribution from ~0 to full
strength over the first ~1 epoch of each step's local training (mirroring
RANKEXT_ORTH_LAMBDA_WARMUP_ENABLED's existing, already-validated-in-this-
codebase ramp for the orth loss coefficient), instead of the current
full-strength-from-batch-1 behavior.

Addresses: DIRECTLY. B_new is zero-initialized every step (nn.init.zeros_),
so the earliest few batches of any step set A_new/B_new's initial direction
from a completely uninformed starting point -- exactly the "no anchor, no
cooperation pressure" window diagnosis.txt 4.3 identifies, and exactly the
window this codebase's OWN existing evidence (the RANKEXT_ORTH_LAMBDA_WARMUP_
ENABLED comment block, citing analysis_rankext_firststep/table4: train_ce_loss
1.5-3.4x higher at local_epoch==1, decaying 2-4 orders of magnitude within
1-2 epochs) already shows is a uniquely volatile transient for a freshly-
added rank_extension block specifically. Unlike candidate 1, this targets
ONLY the volatile early window and returns to full strength well before the
step's training ends, so it does not cap final convergence quality.

Expected magnitude: this is the one candidate with a DIRECT precedent in this
exact codebase for the SAME class of mechanism (a within-step warmup for a
newly-added rank_extension signal) already producing measured, real train_ce
volatility reduction. It cannot be projected to a specific accuracy number
(that would repeat the earlier +30pp-style optimism this task explicitly
warns against) -- but of all 5 candidates, it is the one whose mechanism of
action has the most direct evidentiary support already sitting in this
codebase, at effectively zero cost to final-epoch training quality.

Risk to other 3 variants: LOWEST of the 5. It touches only the new block's
early-training contribution magnitude, not any loss term, not KD, not orth,
not calibration, not the classifier. FactorOrth/KD's own anchoring
mechanisms are undisturbed; if anything, a calmer early transient gives THEIR
anchors (which also act on the new block's output) a less noisy target to
work against. Reuses a formula (orth_lambda_warmup_multiplier) already
proven safe/beneficial in this codebase for a structurally identical
purpose.

Complexity: LOW -- one module-level multiplier read inside
GrowingRankLoRALinear.forward()'s existing new-block branch, one
TrainerCallback (mirrors ClassifierRowRestoreCallback's existing structure),
one new parameter threaded through train_with_trainer() (defaults to None,
i.e. off, for every call site that doesn't pass it -- trivially guarantees
simple_avg is untouched).

================================================================================
CANDIDATE 3 -- damping regularizer between new-block output and
concatenated-old-block output ("a weaker relative of FactorOrth")
================================================================================
Mechanically, as literally described ("penalizing large changes in the
combined output on old-class samples"), this requires OLD-CLASS images
during training. Plain rank_extension trains with replay_per_class=0 -- no
old-class images are present in any active configuration this session is
scoped to touch. The only way to implement this candidate as literally
specified would be to turn on replay (an architecture change well beyond
Task C's scope and explicitly excluded from "no speculative extras").
Reinterpreted onto CURRENT-step data instead (penalizing the new block's own
output magnitude / its interaction with the old block's output, evaluated on
the SAME batch the CE loss uses), this collapses into exactly the machinery
ALREADY implemented and already ACTIVE for a subset of these methods:
compute_delta_orth_components()'s trace/norm/factor_orth terms, wired through
lambda_orth in DeltaOrthRankExtensionTrainer.compute_loss(). This is not a
new candidate -- it is "give plain RankExt (and rank_extension_kd_only_T2,
neither of which currently has lambda_orth>0) some nonzero lambda_orth."

Addresses: directly, and with the STRONGEST existing evidence of the 5
candidates -- rank_extension_orth_factor_lam_50 (lambda_orth=50, i.e. exactly
this mechanism already turned on) is an ALREADY-RUN, in-data demonstration
that this class of fix helps a great deal: restricted step1 57.7% -> 83.2%
(+25.5pp), open all_seen 22.3% -> 34.1% (+11.7pp), vs. plain rank_extension in
the SAME run.

Risk to other 3 variants: this is the candidate's real problem, not its
mechanism. "Apply the SAME protocol to all 4 variants" (pre-authorization #2)
means either (a) giving plain and kd_only_T2 a NEW nonzero orth-like penalty
they don't have today, while the two FactorOrth variants ALREADY have
lambda_orth=50 -- meaning the "same fix" would functionally be a no-op for 2
of the 4 variants and a large change for the other 2 (not actually uniform in
effect, only uniform in mechanism-name), or (b) adding an ADDITIONAL damping
term on top of the existing lambda_orth=50 for the FactorOrth variants,
risking double-regularization/compounding tension with an already-tuned
value never designed to be stacked. Worse: turning plain RankExt and
rank_extension_kd_only_T2 into "RankExt+some-orth" and "RankExt+KD+some-orth"
respectively blurs the four supervisor-selected methods' whole reason for
being four DISTINCT points of comparison (plain / +FactorOrth / +KD /
+FactorOrth+KD) -- this codebase's own commit history repeatedly emphasizes
"one lever per run, isolate cleanly, attribute honestly" as a design
principle; collapsing the plain/FactorOrth distinction directly conflicts
with that principle in a way none of the other 4 candidates do.

Complexity: LOW to reuse the existing machinery, but MEDIUM-HIGH to do so
correctly without double-regularizing 2 of the 4 variants or eliminating the
plain-vs-FactorOrth comparison the experiment is built around.

================================================================================
CANDIDATE 4 -- verify+strengthen classifier-row freezing
================================================================================
Addresses: nothing currently broken. diagnosis.txt sections 1-2 found NO
drift in either the frozen LoRA blocks (bit-exact, directly measured) or the
classifier-row protection mechanism (no drift signature in available
evidence; the mechanism is already doubly redundant by design). Per
pre-authorization #1 ("if Task A finds ANY drift... that IS a bug and takes
priority"): no drift was found, so this pre-authorization's trigger condition
is not met.

Expected magnitude: ~0. Hardening an already-correctly-functioning mechanism
against a failure mode with no evidence of occurring cannot recover any of
plain RankExt's missing accuracy.

Risk to other 3 variants: minimal, but also zero benefit -- pure added
complexity for a problem that does not exist.

Complexity: LOW-MEDIUM (e.g. adding a third, redundant restore point, or an
assertion). Ranked last regardless of low complexity, because complexity is
irrelevant when expected gain is zero -- and pre-authorization #7 explicitly
rules out speculative extras beyond what the tasks require.

================================================================================
CANDIDATE 5 -- feature freezing: consistency loss vs. saved old-block features
================================================================================
Mechanically: save intermediate (post-LoRA-attention) features on old-class
inputs per step, add a loss term penalizing the student's drift from them.
Explicitly acknowledged in the task itself as needing new per-step feature
capture. This ALSO requires OLD-CLASS input images (same rehearsal-data gap
as candidate 3) -- either real old-class samples (replay, out of scope) or a
synthetic/no-image proxy (not specified, not obviously sound). Even before
that gap, it requires: a new feature-cache/replay-buffer subsystem, hooks
into the CLIP encoder to capture intermediate activations, storage/lifecycle
management across 5 sequential per-step Trainer instances, and a new loss
term whose scale relative to CE would itself need tuning from scratch (no
existing precedent in this codebase, unlike candidates 2/3).

Addresses: directly and most completely IN PRINCIPLE (a true anchor against
drift, not just a slower/gentler ramp) -- but only if the old-class-data gap
is solved, which is out of scope this session.

Expected magnitude: unknown; no comparable existing mechanism in this
codebase to project from, and the task's own honesty requirement (pre-auth
#4) means this should not be guessed at.

Risk to other 3 variants: unclear/high -- a brand-new loss term of unproven
scale interacting with existing KD/orth losses (already carefully tuned
against each other, per COMBINED_LOSS_SCALE_ENABLED's own scaling logic
elsewhere in this codebase) is the least predictable of the 5 candidates.

Complexity: HIGHEST of the 5 by a wide margin -- new data pipeline, new
instrumentation, new loss term, no reusable precedent.

================================================================================
RANKING
================================================================================
Scoring (expected gain x probability of success x implementation cleanliness)
/ (risk x complexity), qualitative (no fabricated numeric scores):

  1. Candidate 2 (new-block output warmup)     -- best on every axis: directly
     targets the diagnosed mechanism, has a working precedent IN THIS
     CODEBASE for the same class of fix, lowest risk (touches nothing else),
     lowest complexity, and does not compromise the 4-variant comparison
     design at all.
  2. Candidate 3 (orth-style damping)           -- mechanistically the
     best-EVIDENCED (FactorOrth's real, already-measured +25.5pp/+11.7pp is
     a working example of exactly this idea), but its "apply uniformly"
     framing collapses the plain-vs-FactorOrth distinction and risks
     double-regularizing two of the four variants -- a real design-coherence
     cost candidate 2 does not have.
  3. Candidate 1 (separate lower LR for new block) -- plausible, low
     complexity, but blunter than candidate 2 (slows the WHOLE step, not just
     the volatile opening), with a real, unquantified risk of under-fitting
     new classes.
  4. Candidate 5 (feature freezing / consistency loss) -- highest potential
     ceiling in principle, but blocked on a rehearsal-data gap this session
     is not scoped to close, highest complexity, least predictable risk.
  5. Candidate 4 (harden classifier freezing) -- no problem found to fix
     (pre-authorization #1's trigger condition not met); ranked last
     regardless of its own low complexity.

================================================================================
CHOSEN FIX: Candidate 2 -- new rank block output warmup
================================================================================
Commit, no hedging: implement candidate 2. It is the only candidate that
(a) directly targets the specific mechanism diagnosis.txt identifies (the
new block's uninformed earliest updates perturbing shared computation before
any anchor exists), (b) has a working precedent for the identical class of
within-step warmup already validated in this exact codebase
(RANKEXT_ORTH_LAMBDA_WARMUP_ENABLED), (c) carries the lowest risk to the
other 3 variants of any candidate with a plausible real effect, and (d)
preserves the 4-way plain/FactorOrth/KD/FactorOrth+KD comparison exactly as
designed, applying the SAME mechanism to all four without collapsing any of
them into each other (unlike candidate 3).

Per pre-authorization #2: this fix is applied identically to all 4 variants
even though it plausibly helps plain RankExt the most (it has no other
anchor) and may be closer to a no-op for KD/FactorOrth (which already have
their own, likely-stronger anchors) -- that asymmetry is expected and
acceptable; the alternative (a per-variant hack) would violate the "same
protocol per family" principle this task explicitly protects.
