Indirect evidence is consistent with a real, monotonic scale imbalance:
restricted (step-local) accuracy sits in the 80-97% band at EVERY step for

rank_extension_orth_factor_lam_50_kd_T2: gap -33.5, -49.7, -46.2, -22.3, -3.2
at steps 1-5). That "most-recent-step-wins" pattern is exactly the WA
(Zhao et al. 2020) row-norm-imbalance signature the codebase's own
calibrate_classifier_row_norms() docstring already describes for simple_avg --
but it cannot be quantified NUMERICALLY (e.g. "step 5's rows are Nx larger
than step 1's") from anything saved in R5.

This session adds the missing instrumentation for the next run: FIX 1's
implementation (Task B below) logs
tables/classifier_row_norm_diagnostics_by_method_step.csv --

================================================================================
TASK B -- three fixes for classifier recency bias
================================================================================

B.0 -- the mechanism, restated precisely
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
restricted_argmax_accuracy() masks the argmax candidate set down to a step's
OWN 20 classes before comparing to the label -- so it is, by construction,
INVARIANT to any UNIFORM per-step rescaling of that step's own classifier
rows (multiplying all 20 rows of one step by a positive scalar never changes
which of those 20 rows has the largest masked logit for a given input). The
80-97% restricted accuracy at every step, for every rank_extension variant,
is therefore direct proof the representations are already good at every
step -- what differs between OPEN and RESTRICTED must be inter-step SCALE
competition, not inter-step REPRESENTATION quality. This is the load-bearing
fact behind all three fixes below and behind the Task B dry-run (B.5).

B.1 -- Fix 1: non-destructive, family-aware (regime-grouped) calibration
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Mechanism: rescale each step's classifier row-norms to match the mean norm of
its own TRAINING-REGIME group (not one shared global target mixing
teacher-less step 1 with KD-trained steps 2-N). For non-KD variants there is
only one regime, so this is the SAME operation as the pre-existing "global"
calibration mode (already proven safe for rank_extension's non-KD variants --
see calibrate_classifier_row_norms()'s pre-existing POST-INCIDENT FIX note:
global calibration "corrupted rank_extension's two KD variants... while
leaving its two non-KD variants fine"). For KD variants, step 1 becomes a
singleton group (a no-op -- its untouched, teacher-less rows are never
rescaled using statistics from the differently-trained KD steps, which is the
literal cause of the prior corruption), and steps 2-N are equalized to their
OWN shared mean.
Expected gain: LARGE and specifically targeted. Since restricted accuracy is
scale-invariant (B.0), it is a valid upper bound on open accuracy after
perfect scale equalization. Applying it only to the groups this fix actually
rescales (all 5 steps for the 2 non-KD variants; steps 2-5 for the 2 KD
variants, step 1 left alone) projects all_seen_accuracy upper bounds of
70.6% / 87.0% / 85.3% / 87.8% for rank_extension / rank_extension_orth_
factor_lam_50 / rank_extension_kd_only_T2 / rank_extension_orth_factor_lam_
50_kd_T2 respectively (see projected_improvement.csv, B.5). Even a
conservative 50%-of-gap estimate still projects +12 to +28pp gains across the
4 variants -- the largest realistic gain of the three fixes, because it
attacks the SAME mechanism (row-norm scale) the R5 diagnostic table directly
measured, using a mode already empirically validated as safe for 3 of the 4
affected variants (2 non-KD variants tested clean before; KD variants get a
mechanism specifically designed around the ONE known corruption case).
Risks: (a) mode="global" being accidentally applied to rank_extension instead
of "regime_grouped" would reproduce the KD-variant corruption -- mitigated by
making calibration_mode an explicit, logged, per-family field rather than a
single shared bool, and by keeping simple_avg's own mode/behavior byte-for-
byte unchanged. (b) rank_extension's apply_calibration flips from False to
True for the first time -- this DOES change its "no calibration" status (the
task's framing implies this is expected: the task itself asks to add a
calibration-style fix). (c) purely a post-hoc, eval-time rescale -- no
retraining, no new parameters, no protocol violation (same category of change
as the pre-existing simple_avg calibration, which the run's own docstrings
already treat as a legitimate, standard WA-style correction).

B.2 -- Fix 2: task-ID inference + step-restricted argmax
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Mechanism: train a lightweight step-ID classifier (or reuse features with a
shallow linear probe) and restrict the FINAL argmax to the predicted step's
20 classes, DER-style.
Expected gain: potentially the LARGEST of the three in the limit -- if the
step-ID probe were perfect, this would recover exactly the 80-97% restricted-
accuracy numbers already observed (no upper-bound uncertainty, unlike Fix 1's
ceiling estimate). But that probe is not perfect, and per (A.1) the eval
harness has no other signal it currently keeps around at eval time to build
"is this image from step 3?" training data other than doing a real reserved-
data training run for a NEW classifier head, which is a materially different
protocol from a metrics-only calibration pass.
Risks: this is a NEW mechanism, not a bugfix to an existing one -- it adds a
second model/head, a second training loop, and a step-ID label space that has
to be constructed and validated. It also changes the EVALUATION PROTOCOL
itself (per-step-restricted competition is currently only the DIAGNOSTIC
"restricted" number, explicitly documented in restricted_argmax_accuracy()'s
own docstring as "NOT replacing" the standard open 100-way CIL protocol) --
adopting Fix 2 for the real reported metric would mean rank_extension's
headline number is no longer directly comparable to simple_avg's under the
field-standard open-argmax CIL definition, unless simple_avg gets the same
treatment (out of scope here, and simple_avg does not have this problem to
anywhere near this degree per A.1). This is real DER-style prior art, but it
is the heaviest lift of the three and changes what is being measured, not
just how it's computed.

B.3 -- Fix 3: temperature scaling / logit-norm equalization at inference
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Mechanism: rescale each row's logits by (something proportional to) its own
norm at inference time, per-example, with no training.
Expected gain: SMALL relative to Fix 1. This is really the same lever as Fix
1 (row-norm equalization) but applied per-example/per-forward-pass at
inference instead of once to the persisted classifier weights -- it does not
add a new mechanism, but it also does not add the one piece of engineering
Fix 1 already has for free: TRAINING-REGIME grouping. A naive single global
temperature-per-row-norm rule, applied uniformly, is close to re-deriving the
OLD "global" calibration mode that is already proven to corrupt the KD
variants (same mixing-across-regimes failure mode as B.1's risk (a)) --
unless it is ALSO made regime-aware, at which point it is functionally Fix 1
implemented at a different point in the pipeline (per-example at eval vs.
once on the merged weights), with no accuracy advantage and less favorable
engineering properties (recomputed every forward pass rather than once after
merge; both eval and any downstream deployment code have to remember to apply
it, whereas Fix 1's correction is baked into the shipped weights).
Risks: if implemented naively (global, non-regime-aware), reintroduces the
exact KD-variant corruption documented in calibrate_classifier_row_norms()'s
history. If implemented regime-aware, it is not actually simpler than Fix 1 --
it is Fix 1 with worse locality (applied redundantly at every eval call
instead of once).

B.4 -- Ranking and decision
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Ranked by (expected effectiveness x ease of implementation x protocol
cleanliness):
  1. Fix 1 (regime-grouped calibration) -- large expected gain (backed by the
     restricted-accuracy ceiling numbers below), reuses an existing, already-
     tested mechanism (calibrate_classifier_row_norms) with one new grouping
     rule, is a pure eval-time non-destructive correction (same category as
     the calibration already shipped for simple_avg), and is a one-flag
     revert (RANKEXT_FAMILY_AWARE_CALIBRATION_ENABLED) if it doesn't pan out
     on the next run.
  2. Fix 3 (logit-norm equalization) -- comparable mechanism to Fix 1 but with
     no engineering advantage over it once made regime-aware (which it must
     be, to avoid re-deriving the known-bad global-mixing failure), and worse
     locality (per-forward-pass vs. once on merged weights). Only wins over
     Fix 1 if you specifically want a training-free-AND-merge-free
     correction (e.g. for a serving pipeline that can't re-touch weights) --
     not a consideration here.
  3. Fix 2 (task-ID probe + restricted argmax) -- plausibly the highest
     asymptotic ceiling, but it is a new mechanism (new head, new training
     data/labels, new failure modes for the probe itself) and it changes what
     is being measured (restricted-by-construction is not the same claim as
     open-100-way CIL accuracy), which this analysis is not willing to trade
     for a same-run comparison against simple_avg's untouched open-argmax
     numbers.

CHOSEN: Fix 1 (family-aware, regime-grouped classifier calibration for
rank_extension). Implemented this session, flag-gated via
RANKEXT_FAMILY_AWARE_CALIBRATION_ENABLED (default True for the next run).

B.5 -- Dry run against R5's saved data
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
R5 has NO saved logits or classifier weight VALUES (see A.2) -- only shapes --
so calibrate_classifier_row_norms(mode="regime_grouped") cannot literally be
re-invoked on R5's actual tensors. Instead this dry run uses the B.0 identity
(restricted accuracy is scale-invariant, hence a valid upper bound on open
accuracy after any scale-only correction) applied ONLY to the step-groups
FIX 1 actually rescales for each method (all 5 steps for the 2 non-KD
variants; steps 2-5 only for the 2 KD variants, since step 1 is a deliberate
no-op there) -- see projected_improvement.csv for the full per-step table.
Direction check: YES, strictly positive for every touched step in every
method (by construction, since accuracy_open < accuracy_restricted at every
one of those steps in R5 -- verified, no exceptions among the 18 touched
step-rows out of 20 total rank_extension-family rows). Untouched steps (KD
variants' step 1) are projected at their CURRENT value, i.e. zero claimed
improvement there, consistent with that group being a designed no-op.

Projected all_seen_accuracy (mean of per-step OPEN accuracy, matching how
R5's own all_seen_accuracy column is computed -- see A.1):

method                                   | current | upper bound | delta (UB) | conservative (50% of gap) | delta (cons.)
-------------------------------------------|---------|-------------|------------|----------------------------|----------------
rank_extension                             |   23.89 |       70.59 |     +46.70 |                      47.24 |         +23.35
rank_extension_orth_factor_lam_50          |   30.05 |       86.96 |     +56.91 |                      58.50 |         +28.45

necessarily at the ceiling. Even the conservative estimate (+12 to +28pp
all_seen_accuracy) would be enough to move every rank_extension variant's
ranking materially closer to its simple_avg-family counterpart.
