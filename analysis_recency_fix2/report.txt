A.6 -- why was the +30pp projection so optimistic? diagnosis
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
projected_improvement.csv's methodology (report.txt B.0/B.5 from the prior
session) rests on one identity: restricted accuracy is invariant to any
UNIFORM per-step rescale, so it is a valid CEILING on open accuracy reachable
by scale-only correction. That identity is true, but the projection then
implicitly assumed the ceiling was also close to being REACHABLE -- i.e. that
scale (row-norm) imbalance was the dominant, or a large, component of the
gap. A.5 directly falsifies that for the non-KD variants: Fix 1 achieved
essentially PERFECT row-norm equalization (ratio 1.0000000 across all 5
steps) and open accuracy for the early/middle steps barely moved (rank_
extension step 1: 0.80%->1.15%, step 3: 2.20%->2.95%; rank_extension_orth_
factor_lam_50 step 1: 2.90%->7.70%, step 3: 11.35%->14.00% -- single-digit-pp
moves against a 55-95pp restricted-accuracy ceiling that was, by A.5,
genuinely reachable in row-norm terms). The pre-calibration row-norm spread
itself was also simply too small to explain gaps of this size under any
plausible softmax/logit-competition model: a 15-29% multiplicative norm
difference (non-KD) or <5% and non-monotonic difference (KD) cannot, on its
own, produce 50-80pp swings in a 100-way argmax competition unless the
classifier were operating at an extremely low, ill-conditioned effective
temperature -- there is no evidence of that elsewhere in these runs.
CONCLUSION: the projection's assumption -- "the open-vs-restricted gap is
explained by inter-step mean row-norm imbalance, so closing that imbalance
closes the gap" -- was the specific assumption the actual mechanism did not
deliver on. The gap's true cause lies elsewhere; Task B below identifies what
that "elsewhere" actually is.

B.0 -- the bowl-shaped pattern, restated with Fix 1's real numbers
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
RankExt+FactorOrth+KD (rank_extension_orth_factor_lam_50_kd_T2), Fix 1 run:
  step 1: gap -26.45 (open 68.25, restricted 94.70)  -- Fix 1 helped here (R5: -33.5)

  step 3: gap -40.45 (open 52.95, restricted 93.40)  -- large, modest help (R5: -46.15)
  step 4: gap -23.30 (open 70.05, restricted 93.35)  -- moderate, no help (R5: -22.3)
  step 5: gap  -4.40 (open 92.80, restricted 97.20)  -- smallest (reference step)
Confirmed: NOT "step 5 dominates everything" -- middle steps (2, 3) are the
worst-affected, with BOTH neighbors (step 1 and step 5) doing comparatively
better, despite step 1 never being calibration-touched at all.

B.a -- why do middle steps have worse gaps than step 1, even though step 1
was left untouched (a no-op) while steps 2-3 WERE grouped-rescaled?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Row norms do not explain it (A.5): pre-calibration, step 1's row norm ratio
is by definition 1.000 (it IS the step-1 reference), and steps 2-5's ratios
(1.072, 1.059, 1.104, 1.093 for this method) are all fairly close together --
there is no special "step 2/3 are smaller" or "step 2/3 are larger" signature
in the row norms that would predict step 2/3 being the worst-hit steps by a
magnitude argument. What DOES separate step 1 from steps 2-5 mechanistically:
step 1 is the only step with NO knowledge-distillation loss at all (uses_kd
only starts biting at step 2, since step 1 has no prior-step teacher yet) --
its final-epoch validation CE loss is 0.227, the best of any step in this
method's whole run. Steps 2-5's val_ce_loss (tables/training_loss_history_
by_epoch.csv, final epoch per step) is 0.928, 0.761, 0.752, 0.624 -- i.e. a
sharp JUMP at step 2 (roughly 4x step 1's loss) followed by a MONOTONIC
RECOVERY through step 5. Step 2 is the hardest KD regime the whole run
produces: it is the FIRST step where the student has to balance a real
distillation loss against a teacher that itself only just finished training
(with no KD experience of its own) -- kd_loss_raw shows the same shape
(1.166 at step 2, declining to 0.827 by step 5). By step 5, the model has had
three more steps of practice balancing CE against KD, and both its CE fit and
its distillation quality have measurably improved. Step 1's advantage isn't
that its rows are "left alone" (that's a calibration-time property that
CANNOT causally affect a gap measured against an eval-time model that HAS
already had calibration or not applied); its advantage is that it was never
subject to the hard KD regime at all -- it is trained cleanly, with the best
val_ce_loss of any step. Middle steps 2-3 are the WORST because they are
closest to the point of hardest KD adaptation; step 1 escapes that regime
entirely (different reason), and step 4-5 have had time to recover from it
(same regime, later in the recovery curve).

B.b -- is "steps 2-5 equalized as one group" actually equalizing to a single
target, or was there a bug biasing the target toward step 5 (the largest raw
block)?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
NO BUG. Verified directly from calibrate_classifier_row_norms()'s actual
source (vit_lora_cifar100_full5step_n5.py): group_target_norm is computed as
row_norms[group_idx].mean() over the CONCATENATED row indices of every step
in the group -- since every CL step contributes exactly 20 classifier rows
(CLASSES_PER_STEP is constant), this is mathematically identical to an
UNWEIGHTED mean of the 4 (or 5) per-step mean row norms; no step's block
counts more than any other's regardless of block size/rank/recency.
Confirmed numerically: rank_extension_kd_only_T2's group-B pre-cal norms are
0.6875 (s2), 0.6707 (s3), 0.6732 (s4), 0.6567 (s5); plain mean =
(0.6875+0.6707+0.6732+0.6567)/4 = 0.67203, which matches the logged
post-calibration value (0.672025) to 4 decimals. Same check passes for
rank_extension_orth_factor_lam_50_kd_T2 (plain mean of 0.7249/0.7157/0.7460/
0.7390 = 0.73140, matches logged 0.731381). No step-5 (or any-step) bias.

B.c -- does the gap correlate with row-norm imbalance, or with something else
(training loss residual, block position)?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Row norm: pearson(|gap|, |row_norm_ratio_vs_step1 - 1|) across all 5 steps,
per method (n=5, small-sample caveat applies): rank_extension -0.68,
rank_extension_kd_only_T2 -0.54, rank_extension_orth_factor_lam_50 -0.65,
rank_extension_orth_factor_lam_50_kd_T2 -0.25. Every one of these is NEGATIVE
-- i.e. steps with a LARGER pre-calibration row-norm deviation from step 1
tend to have a SMALLER gap, the opposite sign a "bigger norm mismatch causes
bigger gap" story would predict. This is a confound, not a real inverse
causal relationship: row-norm ratio deviation and step-recency both trend
together (later steps generally drift further from step 1 in norm, per A.5),
and later steps ALSO have smaller gaps simply because they are closer to
(or, for step 5, identical to) the reference/eval step. Row norm is not
carrying independent signal here at all.

Training loss residual (val_ce_loss, final epoch per step): pearson(val_ce,
|gap|) restricted to the group Fix 1's KD calibration actually touches
(steps 2-5 only, n=4): rank_extension_kd_only_T2 = +0.85,
rank_extension_orth_factor_lam_50_kd_T2 = +0.91. Both strongly positive:
harder-to-fit steps (higher val_ce) have larger open-accuracy gaps, and this
holds specifically within the touched group, not just as an artifact of step
position. (Across all 5 steps including step 1, the correlation is weaker/
noisier -- +0.38 and +0.43 -- because step 1 is a qualitatively different,
KD-free regime whose low val_ce does not fully predict its gap; see B.a.)
For the two NON-KD variants, this same check does NOT hold cleanly:
rank_extension's step 3 has the LOWEST val_ce of any step (0.176) yet the
WORST gap (-68.2, worse than step 4's -65.55 despite step 4's val_ce being
the highest, 0.282) -- val_ce does not track the gap for the non-KD variants
the way it does for steps 2-5 of the KD variants.
CONCLUSION: for the KD variants, the gap correlates strongly and
specifically with each step's own training-quality residual (val_ce_loss/
kd_loss_raw) within the calibration-relevant group -- this is a genuine,
usable signal, not row-norm ratio (which is a confound/non-signal here). For
the non-KD variants, NEITHER row-norm ratio NOR val_ce_loss cleanly explains
the observed bowl -- their recency-bias mechanism remains only partially
understood by this session's instrumentation (candidate: rank_schedule
capacity growth itself, since rank grows 16->32->48->64->80 across steps,
giving later steps' blocks more parameters/expressive directions -- not
further pursued here as no matching diagnostic exists yet; flagged for a
future session, not blocking Fix 2 below).

C.1 -- approach selected: confidence-weighted regime-grouped calibration
(KD variants only; provably identical to Fix 1 for non-KD variants)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Of the four candidate directions in the task:
  - Per-step-own-group calibration (each of the 5 steps its own group,
    rescaled to a shared 5-group target): REJECTED. For the non-KD variants
    this is EXACTLY what Fix 1 already does (one group = all 5 steps already
    IS "every step calibrated to a shared target"), and A.6/B.c show that
    lever is already exhausted (perfect equalization, ~0 effect). For the KD
    variants, promoting step 1 into the SAME shared target as steps 2-5 would
    reproduce the exact cross-regime-mixing corruption documented in
    calibrate_classifier_row_norms()'s own POST-INCIDENT FIX history
    (68.0%->26.2%, 59.3%->50.1%) -- there is no version of "make step 1 a
    non-singleton group" that both (a) actually changes anything (it's
    already its own group under Fix 1) and (b) avoids that known failure
    mode. Not adopted.
  - Row-wise (per-class, not per-step-block) equalization: REJECTED. This is
    a FINER-grained version of the same lever (row-norm MAGNITUDE) that A.6/
    B.c just showed is not the dominant driver even at the coarser,
    already-tested block level. There is no reason to expect finer-grained
    magnitude correction to succeed where coarser magnitude correction
    (already near-perfect for the non-KD case) produced ~0 effect.
  - Task-ID inference + step-restricted argmax: NOT adopted this session,
    same reasoning as the prior session's report.txt B.2 -- it is a new
    mechanism (new head, new training data/labels) that changes the
    evaluation protocol itself, not a calibration fix; out of scope for a
    "design a new calibration mechanism" task and not what Task B's
    diagnosis specifically calls for.
  - Confidence-weighted rescaling (target = f(training confidence per
    group)): ADOPTED. This is the ONE candidate that is actually justified
    by Task B's evidence: B.c found the gap correlates strongly (pearson
    +0.85, +0.91) with each step's own val_ce_loss specifically within the
    KD-calibrated group (steps 2-5), and does NOT correlate usefully with row
    norm (confounded, wrong sign). A mechanism that targets the demonstrated
    correlate, instead of continuing to push on the falsified one, is the
    only evidence-backed choice among the four.

Design: calibrate_classifier_row_norms_confidence_weighted() (NEW function,
vit_lora_cifar100_full5step_n5.py) keeps Fix 1's EXACT grouping (KD: {step1}
singleton no-op + {steps2..N} one group; non-KD: all NUM_STEPS steps one
group -- unchanged, per B.a/B.b showing no bug and no evidence step 1's
no-op status was hurting it, so the task's "revert step-1-no-op if it was
hurting step 1" condition is NOT triggered). Within each KD group, instead of
rescaling every step to the flat group mean, each step's target norm is:

    relative_difficulty = step_val_ce / group_mean_val_ce
    boost = clamp(relative_difficulty ** gamma, boost_min, boost_max)   [gamma=0.5, boost in [0.85, 1.30]]
    target_norm = group_mean_row_norm * boost

val_ce is each step's own FINAL-EPOCH validation CE loss, read from the
module-global epoch_loss_rows accumulator (already populated by
EpochValidationCallback during training -- no new training-time
instrumentation needed, this data already exists by the time calibration
runs at the end of run_rank_extension_variant()). gamma=0.5 (sqrt) dampens
the boost so a step with e.g. 2x the group's mean val_ce does not get a full
2x norm target (risk of overshoot: a badly-undertrained step's DIRECTIONS may
also be worse, not just its scale, so aggressively boosting its magnitude
could let it win competitions it doesn't deserve to win for OTHER images);
boost_min/boost_max additionally hard-clip to [0.85, 1.30] regardless of
gamma. A step with no logged val_ce_loss falls back to boost=1.0 (Fix 1's
plain mean-match) -- never a worse-tested state than Fix 1 already validated.

CRITICAL SCOPING DECISION (uses_kd gate): the boost formula is only ever
applied when uses_kd is True. For non-KD groups, boost is unconditionally
forced to 1.0 for every step, making calibrate_classifier_row_norms_
confidence_weighted() numerically IDENTICAL, by construction, to
calibrate_classifier_row_norms(mode="regime_grouped") for rank_extension and
rank_extension_orth_factor_lam_50. This directly follows from B.c: val_ce_loss
does not track the gap for the non-KD variants (rank_extension's step 3
counter-example), so applying the boost there would be acting on noise, with
real risk of active harm (an incorrectly-boosted step could actively win MORE
open-argmax competitions it doesn't deserve to win) rather than just failing
to help. Gating by uses_kd makes Fix 2 provably no worse than Fix 1 for the
two non-KD variants, and confines the new, less-validated lever to exactly
the two variants (and exactly the group: steps 2-5) where Task B's evidence
actually supports it.

C.2 -- structural-integrity / eval-time-only requirements
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Frozen blocks / KD-trained continuity: untouched. The function only
  rescales model.classifier.weight rows (a per-row scalar multiply, in
  torch.no_grad()) on the FINAL merged rank_extension model, after all 5
  training steps have completed -- identical footprint to Fix 1's
  calibrate_classifier_row_norms(), which was already established as
  non-destructive to the frozen rank blocks (they are never touched by
  either function; only model.classifier.weight is).
- Merge/eval-time only: the new function is called from exactly one site,
  run_rank_extension_variant(), at the same point in the pipeline Fix 1's
  call was (right after build_rank_extension_model() constructs the final
  merged model, right before evaluate_model()). No change to any per-step
  training loop, trainer class, or loss function. No new parameters, no
  retraining, no rehearsal data -- same category of change as Fix 1 and the
  pre-existing simple_avg calibration.

SUMMARY
================================================================================
Fix 1 (regime-grouped calibration) achieved its OWN stated goal (equalize
mean row norms within regime groups) essentially perfectly, but that goal
turned out not to be the lever controlling the open-vs-restricted accuracy
gap -- the actual STRICT-vs-Fix1 result was +0.66pp average, one variant
regressed, and 8 of 16 calibration-touched step-rows got WORSE, all far
below the +12 to +57pp range projected. The gap instead has a bowl shape
(worst at steps 2-3) that, for the two KD variants specifically, correlates
strongly (r=0.85, r=0.91) with each step's own training-quality residual
(val_ce_loss) within the calibrated group -- NOT with row-norm ratio (which
is confounded/wrong-signed). Fix 2 (calibrate_classifier_row_norms_
confidence_weighted(), flag-gated via RANKEXT_CONFIDENCE_WEIGHTED_
CALIBRATION_ENABLED, calibration_mode="confidence_weighted_regime_grouped")
redirects the SAME scale-correction mechanism at that demonstrated correlate,
scoped ONLY to the two KD variants (provably identical to Fix 1 for the two
non-KD variants, where the correlate does not hold) -- a small, targeted,
honestly-projected refinement (single-digit pp on the worst-affected steps
of the FactorOrth+KD variant specifically), not a rerun of the earlier
optimism.
