COMMENT 1 -- why factor-orth damages old-class (first_step) performance
--------------------------------------------------------------------------

This turns out to be specific to the simple-average family, not a general
property of the factor-orthogonality penalty. In rank_extension, factor-orth
cannot dilute step 1's contribution through merging because rank_extension
never merges or averages weights at all: each CL step concatenates a fresh,
frozen-once-trained rank block onto the previous ones, and we confirmed
directly from the saved rank-structure logs that these frozen blocks have
exactly zero numerical drift across every later step (max abs weight
difference = 0.0, every step, every run). Consistent with that, factor-orth is
neutral-to-helpful for rank_extension's first-step accuracy in both the old
and reverted runs (+1.5pp / +12.2pp). The real damage is isolated to
simple_avg, where it genuinely does merge independently-trained per-task
adapters by averaging. There, factor-orth's first-step accuracy drop is severe
in the old run (-53pp to -63pp depending on the KD pairing) and the collapse
is oddly all-or-nothing: task 1's own accuracy goes to near-zero while tasks
2-5 stay healthy (72-84%), and task 1's own training loss converges perfectly
fine on its own (factor-orth is correctly inactive during step 1's training,
since there's nothing to be orthogonal to yet) -- so whatever breaks it
happens later, at merge time, when steps 2-5 (which ARE penalized for
overlapping with step 1's subspace) get averaged in. The classifier
calibration and wider target-module changes already in the pipeline cut this
damage substantially (down to -9pp/-11pp in the reverted run) but did not
eliminate it. I can't fully prove the exact merge-time mechanism (does
averaging in the orthogonality-constrained later deltas literally cancel step
1's weight directions, or just inflate their relative scale?) from what's
currently logged -- we don't save per-task adapter weight norms or
cross-task cosine similarity for simple_avg. If we want to close this out
definitively, logging ||dW_t|| and cos(dW_1, dW_t) per target module, before
and after the final merge, for all 4 simple_avg variants, would settle it in
one more run.

COMMENT 3 -- why rank_ext's KD curve rises before it decays
--------------------------------------------------------------------------

Confirmed this is structural and persists in the reverted baseline (2 modules,
no head-LR multiplier, no calibration) -- so it isn't an artifact of the
since-rolled-back 4-module/head-LR-x10 configuration. At every CL step
transition (steps 2 through 5), the weighted KD loss rises 26-35% from the
first to the second epoch of the new step, and that rise lands in exactly the
same epoch where training CE drops fastest (73-78% in that one step). I
checked the teacher wiring and hyperparameters directly in code and config:
the teacher is correctly built from the previous step's frozen state (weights
detached, requires_grad off, logits detached again before the KL term), and
temperature/weight (T=2.0, weight=1.0) are identical to simple_avg's KD
setup -- there's no bug in either. The difference is architectural:
rank_extension keeps its old, frozen rank blocks active in the forward pass
alongside the new step's block, so both write into the same shared weight.
At the start of a new step the model's output is still dominated by the old
(teacher-matching) blocks, so KD starts low; as the new block rapidly learns
the new classes, that update necessarily perturbs the same shared weight the
old blocks depend on, pulling the combined output away from the frozen
teacher just as CE is improving fastest -- then both settle down together for
the rest of the step. simple_avg trains a fully independent adapter per step
with nothing shared to perturb, which is exactly why its KD curve just decays
smoothly instead. In short: the new rank block is "spending" some KD alignment
to buy fast adaptation to new classes, and it pays that back down over the
rest of the step -- not a bug, and a reasonable structural tradeoff given how
rank_extension is built.
