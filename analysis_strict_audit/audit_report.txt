C1. forward_transfer is a hardcoded/placeholder constant, not a computed metric.
    File(s): final_metrics_all_methods.csv, supervisor_selected_accuracy_comparison.csv,
    summary_metrics_selected_methods.csv.
    Value: exactly -0.05 (2 decimal places) for ALL FOUR rank_extension-family
    methods — rank_extension, rank_extension_kd_only_T2,
    rank_extension_orth_factor_lam_50, rank_extension_orth_factor_lam_50_kd_T2 —
    despite first_step_accuracy ranging from 0.8% to 68.95% across these same
    four methods (an 86-point spread). A quantity that is supposed to respond to
    training dynamics cannot legitimately land on the same value to 2 decimals
    across methods this different. It is NaN for all 4 simple_avg-family methods
    (not computed at all for that family).
    Verdict: do not report forward_transfer for this run. It is not evidence of
    anything; it looks like a fallback/default value that was never replaced by
    a real computation for the rank_extension family.

C2. The merge-mechanism "r≈0.95" and "34%/46%/66% survival" claims rest on n=4
    data points and are not uniquely reproducible from one documented formula.
    File: tables/merge_mechanism_by_method_step.csv.
    - Structural fact, confirmed: this table contains rows for ONLY the 4
      simple_avg-family methods (288 rows each = 48 target_modules x 6
      phases, 1152 total). Zero rows for any rank_extension-family method
      (rank_extension does not do a weight-averaging merge, so this is
      structurally expected — but it means any correlation computed from this
      table has at most 4 data points, i.e. 2 degrees of freedom).
    - Recomputed Pearson r between first_step_accuracy and four different
      candidate "merge quality" quantities aggregated per method:
        mean cos(dW1,dWt) post-merge         r = -0.985
        mean ||dW||/||dW1|| post-merge        r = +0.949  (my own recompute: +0.931)
        mean cos(dW1,dWt) pre-merge, step 5   r = +0.960
        mean ||dW||/||dW1|| pre-merge, step 5 r = +0.968
      An r of ~0.93-0.97 IS reproducible — but the SIGN flips depending which
      quantity you pick, and with n=4 (2 df) essentially any 4-point dataset
      with a monotonic-ish relationship will produce |r|>0.9. This is not
      statistically meaningful evidence and must not be presented as if it
      were (e.g. no p-value is meaningful at n=4; a single method reordering
      would likely collapse it).
    - "34%/46%/66% survival": mean post-merge ||dW||/||dW1|| per method =
      simple_avg_factor_orth 0.347 (~34%), simple_avg 0.455 (~46%),
      simple_avg_kd_T2 0.660 (~66%) — three of these do land close to the
      cited percentages. But the 4th method actually in the table,
      simple_avg_factor_orth_kd_T2, is 0.399 (~40%) and is not accounted for
      in any cited figure. Using mean cos(dW1,dWt) post-merge instead gives a
      different set (0.34 / 0.46 / 0.54 / 0.60) that only partially matches.
      No single, precisely-defined column in this table reproduces all three
      cited percentages simultaneously while covering all 4 available methods.
    Verdict: the underlying arithmetic in the table is sound (see OK section),
    but the "r=0.95" / "34/46/66%" narrative as commonly stated overstates what
    4 data points and an under-specified formula can support. Must be
    re-stated with the n=4 caveat and one explicit, named formula before use
    in a thesis, and cannot support any claim about the rank_extension family
    (0 data points there).

M2. Orth-loss warmup is asymmetric between families in a way that is not
    disclosed anywhere, and it materially changes loss magnitudes for the
    "FactorOrth-alone" (no-KD) ablation specifically.
    Source: logs/training_loss_history_by_batch.csv, column effective_lambda /
    lambda_orth_warmup_multiplier, filtered to step=2 (first step after step 1,
    where factor-orth loss first becomes nonzero), epoch<1 (594 batches):
      rank_extension_orth_factor_lam_50        : effective_lambda ramps 0.0 -> 49.92
                                                  across the epoch (verified,
                                                  monotonic, 594 unique values)
      rank_extension_orth_factor_lam_50_kd_T2  : ramps 0.0 -> 49.92 (same)
      simple_avg_factor_orth_kd_T2             : ramps 0.0 -> 14.97 (same pattern,
                                                  scaled to its 15.0 nominal)
      simple_avg_factor_orth (NO KD)           : CONSTANT 50.0 for all 594
                                                  batches — no ramp at all.
    So rank_extension's family-level orth-warmup (rankext_orth_lambda_warmup_enabled)
    applies to BOTH of its factor_orth methods regardless of KD, while
    simple_avg's warmup (combined_orth_warmup_enabled) is scoped ONLY to the
    KD-combined method — the plain simple_avg_factor_orth method gets the full
    lambda=50 hit from batch 1 of every step transition with no cushioning.
    This is a real, verified asymmetry (not the same finding as a first-pass
    subagent check that mis-filtered epoch==1 exactly and wrongly concluded
    simple_avg's warmup "doesn't fire at all" for the KD-combined method too —
    that check was re-run here with epoch<1.0 and the KD-combined method's
    ramp is confirmed present and correct).
    Consequence: this is very likely a real, non-trivial contributor to why
    simple_avg_factor_orth shows an extreme transient loss spike
    (mean_train_factor_orth_loss_weighted = 181.4, single-epoch peak 3230 at
    step 5 epoch 1 — see tables/loss_components_summary_by_method.csv and
    training_loss_history_by_epoch.csv) that rank_extension_orth_factor_lam_50
    does not (peak ~1.0). Part of that 180x gap is a genuine algorithmic
    difference (rank-extension only introduces a small new rank block each
    step vs. simple_avg's full new 80-rank adapter), but part of it is this
    undisclosed, unequal warmup treatment. hyperparameter_consistency_notes.txt's
    blanket claim that methods "use the same main hyperparameters when matched
    by family" is not true of this warmup behavior and should be corrected or
    the FactorOrth-alone cross-family comparison should be flagged as
    confounded by it.

M5. rank_extension and rank_extension_orth_factor_lam_50 report IDENTICAL
    step-5 accuracy (96.95%, to 2 decimals) despite differing everywhere else
    (steps 1-4 differ by 2 to 30 accuracy points between them).
    File: per_step_accuracy_by_method.csv / plots/per_task_accuracy_heatmap.png.
    Root cause, verified directly: rank_extension-family step-1 training is
    BIT-IDENTICAL across all 4 rank_extension variants — confirmed via
    training_loss_history_by_epoch.csv, train_ce_loss and val_ce_loss for
    cl_step==1 match to 0.0 absolute difference across all 4 methods, every
    one of the 9 epochs. This is expected and correct (at step 1 there is no
    KD teacher yet and no prior adapter to be orthogonal to, and
    apply_calibration=False uniformly for this family, so all 4 variants
    literally run the same computation at step 1). Given that, and that
    orth-loss magnitude for this family is tiny (raw ~0.02, see M2), both
    non-KD rank_extension variants converging to the same ~97% ceiling
    accuracy on step 5's own (freshly-trained, unforgotten) classes is
    plausible rather than a caching bug — but this should be spot-checked
    against raw per-class logits before being presented as a finding, since as
    reported it is indistinguishable from an evaluation-caching bug.
    (Corollary: this same step-1 determinism is why best_validation_ce ties
    exactly across KD-method pairs within each family at cl_step=1 — e.g.
    0.196774 shared by rank_extension_kd_only_T2 and
    rank_extension_orth_factor_lam_50_kd_T2 — this is a structural
    consequence of M5, not independent evidence of anything.)

M6. simple_avg-family step-1 training is NOT bit-identical across its 4
    variants, even though the same "nothing but CE is active yet" logic that
    produces exact determinism for rank_extension (M5) should apply equally
    here.
    File: training_loss_history_by_epoch.csv, cl_step==1.
    Max spread across the 4 simple_avg variants at a given epoch:
    train_ce_loss up to 0.0108, val_ce_loss up to 0.0636 (e.g. epoch 1:
    simple_avg=1.171160 vs simple_avg_kd_T2=1.167779). This is small in
    absolute terms but structurally unexplained: if KD and factor-orth are
    truly inactive at step 1 (as their raw loss columns confirm — 0.0 KD loss
    and 0.0 factor-orth loss for every simple_avg-family method at step 1),
    all 4 variants should be running the identical CE-only computation on
    identical data with the identical seed, and should therefore match
    exactly, as rank_extension's variants do. The most likely explanation
    given the config (apply_calibration=True for the whole simple_avg family,
    vs. False for rank_extension) is that per-variant calibration or
    auxiliary-component setup (KD teacher instantiation, factor-orth
    reference storage) consumes RNG state differently even in epochs where
    its associated loss term is mathematically zero — but this is inferred
    from the data pattern, not confirmed from source code, and should be
    checked before being dismissed as harmless.

VERDICT
================================================================================
The core continual-learning accuracy numbers in this run — all_seen_accuracy,
first_step_accuracy, later_steps_accuracy, and their cross-file consistency —
are internally sound: they reconcile exactly from raw per-step data for all 8
methods, are byte-identical across every file that repeats them, and the loss-
decomposition arithmetic underlying the headline figures checks out to
floating-point precision across all 360 method x epoch rows. That part of the
run is trustworthy as the basis of accuracy tables and the redesigned
per-family loss decomposition figure. It is NOT yet trustworthy as-is for
three things that must be fixed or explicitly caveated before use in a
thesis: (1) forward_transfer must be dropped or recomputed — it is a
constant, not a metric (C1); (2) the merge-mechanism "r=0.95" /
"34/46/66% survival" story must be re-stated with its true n=4 sample size and
one explicit, named formula, and must not be extended to the rank_extension
family, for which zero merge-mechanism data exists (C2); (3) the
supervisor-facing method-mapping files must be corrected to show the actually-
applied (post-scaling) hyperparameters for simple_avg_factor_orth_kd_T2, not
the pre-scaling requested ones (C3). In addition, the stale delta_trace
references (M1), the undisclosed asymmetric orth-warmup between families
(M2, which materially affects the "FactorOrth-alone" cross-family
comparison), the silently-overloaded kd_gain/orth_gain column semantics (M3),
and the two misleading plots (M8, M9) should all be fixed before the run is
used to generate final thesis tables/figures — none of these require
re-training, all are fixable from the existing logs and reporting code. With
those five items addressed (C1-C3, M1, M2) and the remaining MAJOR/MINOR
items disclosed as footnotes, this run's data is fit to serve as the basis of
thesis tables and figures; as currently packaged, it is not, because at least
three numbers a committee would plausibly quote directly from these files
(forward_transfer, the merge r/percentage claims, and the combined-variant
hyperparameters) are wrong or unsupported as stated.
